    Get started

    GOV.UK Forms is a new platform that makes it easy to create accessible online forms for GOV.UK.

    1. 1. Check if GOV.UK Forms is right for you

      You can use GOV.UK Forms if you’re from a central government organisation and need to collect information from people for a government service.

      Find out more about the features of GOV.UK Forms.

    2. 2. Create a trial account

      If you want to try it out you can create a trial account.

      You’ll need your government email address.

      Create a trial account

    3. 3. Try it out

      With your trial account you can make a form to see how it works and what it can do.

      You can preview, complete and submit a test form. You can also see what an email with the completed form’s information will look like.

      You cannot make a form live with a trial account.

    4. 4. Get an editor account

      If you’ve tested GOV.UK Forms and you want to use it for your service, you can ask to upgrade to an editor account. With an editor account you can make forms live.

      You, or someone from your organisation, will need to agree to a memorandum of understanding (MOU) if your organisation is not using GOV.UK Forms already.
